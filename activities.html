Semen Production

NDDB launched semen station in 1976 which has grown to become a pioneer in the field with a technologically advanced processing laboratory. During 2021-22, the station produced 14.5 million semen doses and sold 19.2 million semen doses, which are the highest for any semen station in the country. Semen doses were also exported to countries like Vietnam, United Arab Emirates, Nepal, Egypt, Kenya and Bangladesh.

Over the last four decades, Sabarmati Ashram Gaushala has remained the major supplier of quality frozen semen doses of indigenous breeds in the country. The semen doses are supplied to agencies involved in improvement and multiplication of these breeds. During 2021-22, the organization produced and sold around 33.4 lakh semen doses of different indigenous breeds.

Embryo Production

Alamadhi holds 358 acres of land, out of these 145 acres of lands are utilised exclusively for fodder production to meet the nutrient requirement of animals. A total of about 95 hectares of irrigated land is cultivated for growing different fodder crops like Sorghum, Maize, Guinea Grass, cowpea, Hedge lucerne CO4, CO5, Murungai, Subabul, Stylo etc. to cater to the requirement of good quality roughages for the herd round the year. The preservation of the forage crops as Silage and Hay is also done to meet the demand during the lean season.

The economics and viability of an animal production system largely depends on its fodder production programs. Better resource management and farm mechanization have led to an increase in the fodder yield, despite the challenges posed by adverse climate, soil and water salinity.

Almost all the operations in Fodder Farm like ploughing, seedbed preparation, fertilizer broadcasting, inter-cultivation, harvesting and chopping of green forage are mechanised. Whereas, irrigation of fodder crops is done with sprinklers and rain guns, which helps in economizing the use water.
